The Quick Freezing

April 22,2017

Freezing as a food preservation process gained importance as it slows down metabolic processes like enzymatic activities in food products and also microbial growth.

For long term storage and preserving the aesthetics and nutritional value of the product, freezing has to be proper. Two types of freezing processes are prevalent: slow and fast freezing. Difference between these two is:

The size of ice crystals formed in both the cases. Larger ice crystals are formed in case of slow freezing, which damages the cell walls of food materials. On the other hand, smaller ice crystals are formed in case of quick freezing, without much effect on cell walls. Hence, fast freezing is preferred.

Individual Quick Freezing (IOF) is a type of fast freezing process. In this process, food products are frozen quickly as individual particles, hence the quality of the product is maintained. Peas and corns are generally frozen by this method.

Good about "Irradiating Foods"

April 22,2017

It’s a common perception that radiations are always harmful, may it be from sun or from any radioactive metals. These radiations are harmful for our skin and should definitely be avoided. On the other hand, these radiations can be put to some good use by irradiating food materials as it kills the harmful microorganisms. And no harmful products remain.

In this process, the food is passed through the irradiation field, so the food itself never becomes radioactive, and it completely safe to consume. Contamination might occur only if it comes in contact with the radioactive metal, which won’t be the case as the facility is made completely safe.

Also, this is a low temperature technology, doesn’t raise the temperature of the food product. Also, the appearance and taste of the food product remains the same. The unit for measuring the dose of radiation is "Gray". A dosage of 2-7 kGy is generally sufficient to eliminate pathogenic organisms from a food product.

There are many advantages of using this technology. Spices are usually preserved using this process. The preservation of wine from unwanted bacterial that could spoil the wine, can also be done using this technology, but this isn’t done commercially, and still in the trial stage of research.

Hence, it is very important to change our perspective and adapt ourselves with the latest technologies emerging in the world, because such technologies would only be successful, if we, the consumers, start using such products.
