(Artificial) Sweetne(r)ss: Overloaded

April 22,2017

An average sugar-sweetened soda bottle has approximately 150 calories, and the diet version of it has 0 calories. But ever wondered, by saving on these 150 calories, what negative health impact it could have on the body??

Yes that’s true. Market these days is flooded with zero or low calorie food products. These are made using sugar substitutes, also known as non-nutritive sweeteners, like, aspartame, sucralose, saccharin, sugar alcohols, etc. The claims regarding the health effects of these sweeteners are quite confusing, as some saying these to be helpful, while other declaring these to be harmful for the body.

Recent studies show that these could potentially be harmful for the body and could further lead to several other health problems, including:

  • excessive weight gain
  • metabolic syndrome
  • diabetes
  • cardiovascular diseases
  • hypertension

Although, stevia, has recently been used commercially, is a natural sweetener and is derived from a plant. As far as safety aspects are concerned, no negative effects related to the consumption of stevia have been reported so far.

So next time you sip in a diet drink or consume a diet food product, do keep in mind the potential health hazards these products could cause. Stay healthy, stay fit !!

Reference: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20303371

Brown Rice: The Healthier Version

April 22,2017

It is an old saying that All that glitters is not gold, and goes aptly with the rice. The shiny appearance of white rice could be deceiving. On the other hand, not so good looking brown rice is more nutritious. Most of the time we go by the looks. But in case of rice, don’t get deceived by the shiny appearance. The polished rice is shiny, and is less nutritious than the brown rice.

The difference between the two types of rice is the different processing techniques. The crude form of rice is brown, in which just the outer layer of hull is being removed. Brown rice is further processed or milled into white rice by removing its outer coating of bran. This process, not just removes the coat, but also the nutrients contained in the coat, including thiamine, magnesium, potassium and magnesium.

Hence, white rice is devoid of these vitamins and minerals, which are present in brown rice. Thiamine, the vitamin present in rice is destroyed during milling and polishing of brown rice to white rice. Moreover, brown rice is considered as a low glycemic index food product, hence it reduces the risk of type 2 diabetes.
So why not give it a try and switch to consuming the healthier version of rice.

Healthy Bacteria !!!

April 22,2017

The market these days is flowing with food products comprising probiotics, the healthy bacteria, which impart health benefits to the consumers. Probiotics are live microorganisms can be ingested, thus improving the gut flora of the host. These mainly comprises organisms of genera, Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium. The major advantage of consuming them is: improving host’s immune system, better calcium absorption, alleviating diarrhoeal symptoms, improving symptoms of lactose intolerance, better digestion and adding bulk to the stool.

Various forms of probiotic food products available in the market are yogurt, fruit drinks, etc. Majorly consumed yogurt can be made even more healthy option by the addition of fruit pulp to it. Probiotics are sensitive to external environmental stresses, like high temperature and low pH. Hence, these are encapsulated to increase their viability in the body of the host. Food grade polymers like, CMC, gelatin, alginate are used for this purpose.

It is quite important to stay fit and healthy, so that we can enjoy life and eat whatever we want. One step towards this would be improving the gut flora. Whenever we are on a course of antibiotics, which kill the probiotics in the body, it is always recommended to consume probiotics after the course is complete, to recover back the gut flora.

Seaweeds: The next Gen Food

April 22,2017

Quite recently seaweeds have been recognized as a food for good health. It is nutrient-rich food product due to a high mineral and protein content. Edible seaweeds are typically members of the algae family grown in marine sources, which are known in three varieties: red, green and brown. The seaweed varieties that are generally consumed include: Nori, Kombu, Dulse, Wakame.

Seaweeds can be consumed in the form of raw salad added with ingredients like vinegar, lemon for better taste. The most important ingredient it conatins is iodine, which is missing in almost every other food product.

Seaweeds can be considered as superfoods because of their low cost and are highly nutritious food products. Following are the benefits of consuming these products:

  • Proteins – It contains all the essential amino acids, especially, glycine, arginine, alanine and glutamic acid. Spirulina, kind of a famous seaweed, contains 70% protein content on dry weight basis.
  • Lipids – It contains essential fatty acids, which may form a part of balanced diet.
  • Vitamins – Water soluble vitamins are present, like B & C, and fat soluble vitamins, like A and E. These are also considered a good source of vitamin B12, which is generally not found in land plants.
  • Minerals – Iron and copper source and also magnesium. Also, rich in calcium and available as calcium phosphate, which is more available than the milk calcium (calcium carbonate).
  • Dietary Fibre – not digested by the digestive enzymes, hence help in the growth of microflora (probiotics) in the colon.

Rich in phytochemicals and enhance digestive health, and overall human nutrition. Provides low calorie value to the diet. So why not give it try and include the seaweeds in your daily diet to get proper nutrition.
