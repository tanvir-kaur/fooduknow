Lactose Intolerance: A digestive disorder

April 22,2017

Do you always feel uneasy and suffer from digestive symptoms after having milk and milk products? Have you ever got suggestions from people around you like you might have a milk allergy? So lets know what this actually is.

You might think lactose intolerance is a type of milk allergy? No, that isnt correct. In milk allergy, immune response occurs due to milk proteins, while lactose intolerance is a digestive disorder. People with milk allergy cant even tolerate the minor level of milk proteins. On the other hand, lactose intolerants could tolerate up to 12 g of lactose per day and also, dairy products like hard cheese which are processed, henceforth containing less lactose as compared to milk.

Lactose is a sugar present in milk and our body cant absorb it as such through bloodstream. Hence, we have lactase enzyme that digests lactose and converts it into absorbable form, like, glucose and galactose. But, deficiency of this enzyme makes body unable to digest lactose, leading to a digestive disorder, known as lactose intolerance. This deficiency of enzyme varies from individual to individual, so as tolerance level of lactose.

Now, the question arises how would you know whether you are lactose tolerant or not? So, whenever you are drinking milk and milk products and come across the following symptoms after 30 minutes to 2 hours, you should consult doctor and get it tested via Hydrogen breath test & Stool acidity test.

  • Abdominal cramps & pain
  • Nausea
  • Bloating
  • Excessive flatulence (gas)
  • Stomach rumbling

Every lactose intolerant person needs to look for ways to avoid such digestive symptoms, as there is no cure to it. Here are few of them-

  • Avoid milk and milk products.
  • Avoid the products that may contain lactose such as salad dressing, sauces, processed breakfast cereals, bakery products, soup, protein powders and bars, non-dairy whipping cream.
  • Use alternative milk like soy milk, rice milk, coconut milk, almond milk, hazelnut milk, oat milk, peanut milk.
  • Sometimes you can also find medications, like lactase in tablet or drug form.

Avoiding of dairy product couldnt fulfill the need of calcium and vitamin D which is necessary for bones. Hence, it is recommended to use products, such as Spinach, Sardines, Rhubarb, Soy milk, Broccoli, Orange, Tuna, Lettuce or its supplementation in medication form, containing calcium or vitamin D.

Next time, check the label while purchasing food product so that you can avoid it if you are lactose intolerant or even milk allergy.
