April 22,2017

Standards of tea, the top commercial crop in India, has come under the purview of FSSAI to maintain the quality and safety of it, while the tea board also regulates the trade activities of tea in India, as well as in other countries.

Lately, green tea has become one of the most demanding teas in India due to its health benefit like antioxidant activity etc. Indian Food Authority has categorized green tea under beverage category and defined it as a product that is exclusively derived from leaves, buds and tender stems of varieties of the species Camellia Sinensis with the aid of processes like enzyme inactivation, rolling and drying. The difference between the different tea types is that black tea is fermented, and on the other hand, green tea is unfermented.

Any extraneous matter, insect, rodent, or harmful substances and colors are not allowed in green tea but it may contain natural flavor or flavoring substances. Probiotics are sensitive to external environmental stresses, like high temperature and low pH. .Hence, these are encapsulated to increase their viability in the body of the host.
