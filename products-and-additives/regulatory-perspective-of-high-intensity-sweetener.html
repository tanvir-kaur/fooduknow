Regulatory Perspective of High Intensity sweetener

April 22,2017

High intensity sweeteners or non-nutritive sweeteners are sugar substitutes, with low calories, which are used in many food and beverage products. FSSAI, USFDA and EU has allowed the use of Acesulfame potassium, Aspartame, Saccharine, Neotame, and Sucralose as food additives in various food products. In the present time, consumers have become quite health conscious and wish to enjoy a low calorie diet. Hence, non-nutritive sweeteners could be used in various food category as per additive regulations in India. Minimum total solid content in carbonated water/sweetened aerated water/fruit beverage/carbonated fruit beverage/fruit nectar is not required if artificial sweeteners are used in them. These artificial sweeteners are not recommended for children

It is mandatory to specify on the label, if a product contains these sweeteners, in following form:

CONTAINS ARTIFICIAL SWEETENERS AND FOR CALORIE CONSCIOUS

This contains………..…….. (Name of Artificial sweetener)

Not recommended for children

Quantity of Sugar added……. gm/100gm or No Sugar added in the product

The artificial sweetener, aspartame releases phenylalanine after ingestion in body, and this amino acid can create health hazards for those having phenylketonuria – a rare inherited disease. Hence, it is not recommended for such a population, and must be mentioned on the label as “Not for Phenylketonurics”.

These table top sweetener may also include filler particles or carriers: dextrose, lactose, maltodextrin, mannitol, sucrose, isomalt, citric acid, calcium silicate, carboxy methyl cellulose, cream of tartar, cross carmellose sodium, colloidal silicone dioxide, glycine, L-Leucine, Magnesium stearate, purified talc, polyvinyl pyrrolidone, providone, sodium hydrogen carbonate, starch, tartaric acid, and erythritol. Also, there are other additives allowed in these sweeteners with limits, as per Indian regulations. European food safety authority (EFSA) concluded that these intense sweeteners are sugar replacers which lower the blood sugar level and reduce the tooth demineralization based on the evaluation of scientific literature to substantiate these claims.
