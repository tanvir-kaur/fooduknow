Probiotic Foods: Foods or drugs in India?

April 22,2017

As the market value of functional food grows, role of probiotics is also become prominent, along with consideration of regulatory structure appropriate to them. FAO/WHO defined the probiotics as live microorganisms which provide health benefits to the host when administered in adequate amount. Probiotics could classify under various categories depending upon their intended use by regulatory body of that country.

Country Category Regulatory Body
India Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, Food with added Probiotic ingredients, Food for special dietary uses, Food for medical purposes FSSAI
USA Dietary Supplements, Drugs, Live Biotherapeutic agents/medical food DSHEA, FDA, BLA
EU Food Supplements, Novel Foods EFSA
Japan Functional Foods, Nutraceuticals FOSHU, MHLW
Australia/New Zealand Functional Foods FSANZ
Canada Natural health foods Natural Health Products Directorate

Now, lets have a look into the Indian scenario, where apex regulatory body defined a separate category for probiotic foods namely “Foods with added probiotic ingredients” which reflects that any foods can be constituted with probiotics mentioned in regulations or notified by authority from time to time, except for the commonly used starter culture of lactic acid bacteria used in fermented milk (Dahi) or related products. These functional foods should contain minimum 108 CFU/g, or else FBO has to take approval prior to market them. But the question arises, how do consumers know about probiotic foods? Consumers can easily find out from package label, on which “PROBIOTIC FOOD”, name of probiotic, and there viable numbers are mandatory to be mentioned. These products are “NOT FOR MEDICAL USE”. In conclusion, Probiotic foods are functional foods, not drugs and it could confer the health benefits on host when taken in adequate quantity and are safe to be consumed based on scientific studies and standards set by FSSAI.
