April 22,2017

Most of chocolates in India are imported and has vegetable fats along with cocoa butter in them and are known as “imitation chocolate”; use of vegetable fats is prohibited in Indian standards, but allowed as per CODEX and USFDA. Quite recently, FSSAI has issued a draft to allow vegetable fat up to 5% of finished products, excluding the weight of other edible foodstuffs (like nuts), but with a minimum content of cocoa material in chocolate. Additives of following functional class like artificial sweeteners, emulsifiers, stabilizers, sequestering and buffering agents are allowed as per additives standards.

So the next time you enjoy a chocolate, you would definitely consider the ingredients and calories you are consuming.
