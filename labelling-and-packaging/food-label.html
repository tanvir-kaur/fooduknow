Food Label: A mirror of Product

April 22,2017

What is a food label? It is a means to advertise the product quality, to target specific consumer and to make brand value. A label of packaged food is the only way of communication between Food business operators (FBO) and consumers. FSSAI, the apex food regulatory body of India provides certain guidelines regarding packaging and labelling.

Complete information has to be displayed on principal display panel (it include all visible side of package) in legitimate form. FBOs have to mention following information of the food products on the label ( deviating from these regulations leads to misbranding the product resulting in penalty ):

  • Product name & its nature
  • Ingredient’s description – all constituents of the product
  • Declaration of Food additives like flavours, colours etc
  • Logo to denote whether the product is from vegetarian (Green dot) or non-vegetarian source (Brown dot). (Exceptions include: Water, carbonated water, alcoholic drink, Milk, and milk powder)
  • Facts which reflect the nutritive value of product like Energy (kcal), Protein (g), Fat (g), Carbohydrate along with sugar (g) etc.
  • Detail of FBO, like name and complete address of manufacturer or importer or packer and country of origin for imported products
  • The net quantity of the product inside the package
  • Date by which product will be safe to consume, in term of best before or expiry date or use by date
  • Batch no or code no to trace back or recall the product easily if required
  • Manufacturing or packaging date of product in term of days, month or years depending on shelf life of products
  • Direction of use, if necessary

So next time you visit a shop to purchase any product, do read the Food Label, so that you are not mislead by just the attractive packaging.
