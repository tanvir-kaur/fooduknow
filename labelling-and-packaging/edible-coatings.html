Edible Coatings: The Silver Food Wrappers of Food Industries

April 22,2017

Have you ever wondered why some apples are more shine in appearance than others? Are they really fresh apples? Is it wax coating on Apples? Is it safe to use these types of Apples? And continue. Yes, these shining on apples are due to wax coating over it. Apples produce wax coating over it naturally but 50% of it depleted during handling, transportation etc. Hence, Producer wraps up it with other wax coating separately. So let’s face the truth and facts about edible coatings here.

These type of thin layer coatings made of edible material that applied to surface of food product to provide a barrier to oxygen, moisture, or in short, you can say to extend the shelf life of product are known as edible coatings. Following are the distinct features that make edible coatings different from any other synthetic wrapper or packaging:-

  • Non-toxic and non-allergic
  • Contain edible components
  • Structure integrity and mechanical barrier
  • Water resistant, moisture barrier, gas barrier
  • Biochemical and microbial stable
  • Serve as carrier for additives, flavor, color, vitamins, minerals or enzymes etc
  • Easily manufactured and cost effective
  • Batch no or code no to trace back or recall the product easily if required
  • Biodegradable and environment friendly

Edible coatings can be wrapping up on food items by several methods like brushing, spraying, dipping and panning followed by drying. But are really they made up of edible materials or with synthetic polyfilm? Yes, it constitute of basic biomolecules such as Lipids, Carbohydrates and Proteins with distinct functionality of it as –

  • Carbohydrates: It act as good oxygen barrier and create the anaerobic environment inside food which increase the shelf life of product but it is more susceptible for moisture due to its hydrophilic nature. Examples: Chitosan, Alginates, Carrageenans, Cellulose, Starch and its derivatives.
  • Proteins: Provide mechanical strength due to its polymerization of amino acids but prone to moisture absorption due to hydrophilic nature. Examples: Gelatin, Corn Zein, Soy Protein, Casein, Keratin, Wheat Gluten, Casein, Collagen, Whey.
  • Lipids: Good barrier to moisture due to its hydrophobic nature but brittle in strength. Examples: Wax and oil based coatings (Animal wax – Bees wax, shellac, spermaceti wax; Vegetable wax –Carnauba wax, Bayberry wax,, palm wax; Mineral and synthetic wax- Paraffin wax, microcrystalline wax), fatty acids, Resins, Emulsions.

As we have seen, not an individual molecule is effective to form edible coatings. Hence, composite or bilayer edible coatings is most preferable in which could be combinations of any 2-3 biomolecules. Edible coatings are also available on commercialized French fries, cheese strips, fruits and vegetables etc.

People generally think that these edible coatings are not safe but it’s not the case. Regulatory authorities USFDA and FSSAI allowed wax coatings over fruits and consider it as safe.
