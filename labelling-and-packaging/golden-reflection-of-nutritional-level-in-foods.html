% DV or % RDA: Golden reflection of nutritional level in foods

April 22,2017

Whenever you are purchasing any package food, you will notice that % Daily value (DV) or % Recommended Dietary Allowances (RDA) are mentioned on the label. Have you ever thought how significant it is and what does it mean? Here, we will provide a brief insight on this.

For example, amount of sodium (280 mg) is mentioned on the label, but is it really relevant to you? No, but the % DV of sodium (12%) provides the requirement of sodium in one serving; so that you can consume that food product according to your diet.

Still confused? Let me drive into some technical information. Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) or Reference Daily intake (RDI) is the average daily dietary nutrient intake level that meets the requirement of healthy individuals. RDA is determined based on multiple factors like age group, gender, and physiological status of individual and group. It has set by ICMR & NIN guidelines in India and it is mandatory to mention %RDA on the label, in case of health supplements, nutraceuticals, food for special dietary uses and food for special medical purposes by FSSAI, but not for normal food products in India. On other hand, % DV is necessary to be mentioned on the label for all food products in U.S. and Canada and is determined based on RDI or DRV (Daily reference value) and average diet of 2000 calorie. % Daily value or %RDA is calculated based on the ratio of amount of nutrient in a serving of food and RDA or DV of that nutrient. For example, assume that amount of Calcium in Food is 300 mg per serving and RDA of Calcium is 600 mg/day (as per ICMR), then %DV of calcium would be 50% and you have to take 2 servings of the product to meet 100% daily value of calcium in a day.

So, now you might have thought that is this necessary to aim for 100% DV for all nutrients? It all depend on nutrients and thatâ€™s why FDA categorized them into 3 categories:

  • Nutrient to encourage:include Dietary fiber, Vitamin D, Calcium, Potassium, Iron (mandatory to mention) and other also like Vitamin A and C (voluntarily basis) and these nutrients are encourage to aim for more than 100% DV.
  • Nutrient to limit: include total fat, saturated fat, trans-fat, cholesterol, and sodium and 100% DV is limit to consume these nutrients as it may cause other effects if consume more.
  • Nutrient without comment: include total carbohydrate, sugar and protein and these not regarded as health concern except added sugar.

Sometime, you will find nutrients with more than 100% DV on label and it is due to presence of various RDI or RDA set by different authorities (FDA, Health Canada, ICMR etc).

So, next time when you purchase any food pre-package foods, just go through the nutritional panel to know how healthy it is for you.
